Best digital art software for beginners in 2025?

Hello everyone!

I'm looking to get into digital art but don't know which software to start with. Any recommendations for someone with no prior digital experience but some traditional art background? I've been drawing with pencils and watercolors for a few years, but I'd like to explore digital options.

I'm not sure if I should go for something free to start with or invest in professional software right away. My budget is somewhat flexible, but I'd prefer not to spend a ton of money until I know I'll stick with it.

Also, would a drawing tablet be necessary from the beginning, or can I start with just a mouse? If tablets are recommended, any suggestions for beginner-friendly options?

Thanks in advance for any advice!

Welcome to the digital art world, MichaelArt! Great to hear you're looking to make the transition from traditional media.

For beginners in 2025, I'd recommend starting with Krita or Clip Studio Paint. Krita is completely free and has really matured over the years with an intuitive interface that traditional artists find comfortable. Clip Studio Paint has an affordable one-time payment option and is specifically designed for illustrators with amazing brush engines.

If you're on iPad, Procreate remains the gold standard in 2025 - it's a one-time purchase of around $12 and incredibly powerful.

As for tablets, I strongly recommend getting at least a basic drawing tablet from the start. Working with a mouse for digital art is possible but extremely limiting and frustrating. The Wacom Intuos line is still reliable for beginners, and XP-Pen has some very affordable options with good quality. The new ArtCanvas 12 that just came out is getting rave reviews for its price point.

Hope this helps!

Adding to what DigitalArtPro said, I'd like to mention Adobe Fresco which has improved dramatically in 2025. It bridges traditional and digital techniques really well with its live brushes that simulate watercolor and oils. They've added some amazing features in the recent updates.

There's also Corel Painter 2025 which feels most like traditional media but has a steeper learning curve.

As for tablets, if your budget allows, I'd recommend looking at pen displays where you draw directly on screen - the newer Huion Kamvas Pro series offers incredible value for money now, especially their mid-range models with excellent color accuracy.

One thing to consider in 2025 is that many programs now have excellent AI-assisted tools that can help beginners with tasks like symmetry, perspective, and even color selection. The ArtIntel features in many programs are actually helpful rather than gimmicky now!

I'm going to go against the grain here and suggest starting with free options before investing anything substantial. You can get surprisingly far with completely free tools these days!

FireAlpaca is super lightweight and easy to learn. MediBang Paint is also free and excellent for illustration. Both will run on older computers without issues.

For tablets, before spending money, you might want to try using your smartphone or tablet with a stylus if you already own one - there are adapter apps now that let you use them as drawing tablets connected to your computer. The new ConnectDraw app is surprisingly good for this.

Once you're sure you enjoy digital art, then investing in better tools makes sense. Just my two cents as someone who likes to minimize initial costs!
